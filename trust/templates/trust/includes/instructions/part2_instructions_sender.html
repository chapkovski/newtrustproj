Estimation Task for Person A

Person A has to estimate how many Token the paired Person B will return if Person A would transfer his initial endowment, independently of whether Person A has actually transferred his endowment.

Since Person A at this moment does not know yet what city Person B is from, he will have to make his estimate for each of the 12 cities that are involved in this experiment.

Remember: Before making her decision, Person B was not informed about whether Person A has actually transferred his endowment, or not.

Therefore, Person B had to decide how many out of the 30 Token she would have returned to Person A in case Person A had chosen to transfer his endowment to Person B.

It is, therefore, possible for Person A to estimate the number of Token Person B has stated to return.

Payoff calculation for Person A

Person A’s estimate of Person B’s return decision is compared with the actual return decision that the paired Person B has made in Part 1 of the session.

The estimate is converted into a payoff according to the following rule:

Difference between the estimate of Person A and Person B’s actual decision: Person A’s Payoff in Part 2:
The estimate of Person A coincides with Person B’s decision: 20 Tokens
The difference is larger than 0 but not larger than 3 Tokens 10 Tokens
The difference is larger than 3 Tokens 0 Tokens
